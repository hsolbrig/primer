The Shape Expressions (ShEx) language describes RDF graph structures. A shape describes the triples involving nodes in RDF graphs. These descriptions identify predicates and their associated cardinalities and datatypes. ShEx shapes can be used to communicate data structures associated with some process or interface, generate or validate data, or drive user interfaces.

This document introduces ShEx to people familiar with basic concepts of RDF and SPARQL. This primer develops examples from an issue tracking application to introduce all major features of ShEx, including the construction of shapes and their correspondence with RDF graphs. This primer is a possible companion to the langauge specifation that serves as a language reference and full specification.
